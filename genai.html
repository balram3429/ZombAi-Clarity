Generative AI

From GANs to diffusion models — how AI learned to create images, music, video, and more from scratch.

🎨 What Makes AI "Generative"?

Discriminative models classify or predict. Generative models learn the distribution of data and can sample new examples from it. They answer: "Given what I've learned, what could a new example look like?" This enables creation of images, text, audio, and video.

⚔ GANs — Generative Adversarial Networks

Introduced by Ian Goodfellow in 2014, GANs train two networks against each other: a Generator that creates fake data, and a Discriminator that tries to tell fake from real. This adversarial game pushes both networks to improve until the generator creates indistinguishable fakes.

GAN Training Loop — Generator vs Discriminator
Generator: noise z → fake image x̃
Discriminator: image → P(real)
min_G max_D: E[log D(x)] + E[log(1 - D(G(z)))]

🌊 Diffusion Models — The New Paradigm

Diffusion models replaced GANs as the dominant image generation approach (Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, Midjourney). The idea: train a model to reverse a gradual noising process. Add noise step by step to destroy an image, then teach the model to undo each step. To generate: start from pure noise and denoise step by step.

Diffusion Process — Forward (noising) & Reverse (denoising)
→

Forward Process

Gradually add Gaussian noise to an image over T timesteps (T≈1000) until it's pure noise.

←

Reverse Process

A U-Net learns to predict and remove the noise at each timestep, conditioned on a text prompt.

🎯

CLIP Guidance

Text prompts are encoded by CLIP and used to steer the denoising toward prompt-matching images.

🗜 Variational Autoencoders (VAE)

VAEs learn a compressed latent space representation of data. Unlike regular autoencoders, VAEs learn a distribution over the latent space — so you can sample new points and decode them into new data. Latent diffusion models (Stable Diffusion) use a VAE to compress images before diffusing in latent space.

VAE Architecture — Encode → Sample → Decode

🌐 Multimodal AI

Modern AI isn't limited to one modality. Models like GPT-4V, Claude, and Gemini process text + images + audio together. The secret: project all modalities into a shared embedding space, then run a transformer over the combined sequence.
